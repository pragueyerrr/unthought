# Unthought — Session Protocol

*The methodology for collecting prereflective route data from participants. Updated as the method evolves.*

**Current version:** 0.1 — designed for session zero, one participant, informal setting.

---

## Materials

| Item | Purpose |
|---|---|
| 2 sheets blank paper | Route sketching — one per attempt if needed |
| 2–3 pens in different colors | Participant chooses — the choosing is a settling moment |
| Google Earth (earth.google.com) | Route tracing and KML export |
| Voice memo app | Captures narration during phases 2 and 3 |
| Phone or camera | Photographs paper sketch immediately after drawing |
| Session log document | Facilitator notes — open on phone during session |
| `unthought_sessions/` folder | KML storage, named by session number |

---

## The Five Phases

---

### Phase 01 — The introduction
**Duration:** ~5 minutes

The introduction must be just enough to get the participant willing — not enough to prime what the "right" answer looks like. A participant who knows what you're looking for will trace what they think is interesting rather than what is true.

| Step | What to do |
|---|---|
| 1 | Say: *"I'm working on something and I want to try it with you. It involves drawing a route you take regularly — a commute, a walk, anything habitual. Don't overthink it, just trust the first thing that comes to mind."* |
| 2 | Ask them to pick one route they take regularly without thinking about it. |
| 3 | Give ten seconds to decide. The first one that surfaces is the right one. Don't let them deliberate. |

> **Note:** Do not mention data, terrain, layers, or what you're looking for. Let the project reveal itself in phase five.

---

### Phase 02 — The paper sketch
**Duration:** ~10–15 minutes

The paper sketch captures embodied memory — the route as the body knows it, not as the map shows it. Imprecision is not a problem. It is the point.

| Step | What to do |
|---|---|
| 1 | Hand them paper and a pen. Ask them to close their eyes for 30 seconds and feel the path in their body before drawing. |
| 2 | While they draw, stay quiet. Watch where they hesitate, redraw, speed up. These are data. |
| 3 | When finished, ask them to narrate in **first person, present tense**: *"Walk me through what you drew, as if you're walking it right now."* |
| 4 | Start the voice memo at the beginning of the narration. |
| 5 | Photograph the paper sketch immediately. Note anything observed — hesitations, redraws, where they started. |

> **Note:** Present tense is embodied. Past tense is reflective. If they slip into past tense, gently redirect: *"As if you're there right now."*

---

### Phase 03 — The map together
**Duration:** ~15–20 minutes

The map trace captures the route as it actually exists in space. The gap between the paper sketch and the Google Earth trace is one of the most important things you will record.

| Step | What to do |
|---|---|
| 1 | Open Google Earth at earth.google.com. Navigate to their starting point together. |
| 2 | Hand them control of the mouse or trackpad — they trace, you watch. |
| 3 | Use the path/line tool to trace the route. Let them go at their own pace. |
| 4 | Ask questions as they trace — *"Is that how you usually go?"* / *"Did you always take that turn?"* |
| 5 | Note moments where the map surprises them — where memory and actual street diverge. |
| 6 | Save as KML. Name format: `session_[##]_[neighborhood]_[YYYY-MM-DD].kml` |

> **Note:** The gap between the paper sketch and the Google Earth trace is data. Record it explicitly — did they go further, shorter, differently? Did the map correct or contradict their memory?

---

### Phase 04 — The constraint question
**Duration:** ~10–15 minutes

The constraint question is the philosophically rich data point — the structure that shapes movement before the question of movement is ever consciously raised. It requires slowness. It often surfaces through laughter first.

| Step | What to do |
|---|---|
| 1 | Wait until the route is fully traced and saved. Ask while you're both still looking at the map. |
| 2 | Ask: *"Is there anything along this route, or nearby, that you always avoid or always move toward without really deciding to?"* |
| 3 | Give it 20–30 seconds of silence. Do not fill the silence. |
| 4 | If they ask what you mean: *"Anything — a building, a street, a turn. Something that shapes how you move without you consciously deciding it."* Do not give examples. Examples prime answers. |
| 5 | Whatever surfaces — follow it. This is the richest part of the session. |

> **Note:** The laugh often comes right before the honest answer. Wait through it.

---

### Phase 05 — The reveal and conversation
**Duration:** Open

| Step | What to do |
|---|---|
| 1 | Tell them what Unthought actually is. Show them unthought.io. Let them read. |
| 2 | Watch their reaction — specifically what they ask first. That is what the project still hasn't made clear enough. |
| 3 | Ask: *"What did it feel like to do that?"* Not what they thought of the project — what the experience itself felt like. |
| 4 | Stop the voice memo. Everything after this is conversation, not data collection. |

---

## Facilitator debrief

*Write these down immediately after the participant leaves. Memory degrades fast.*

| Question | Notes |
|---|---|
| Where did they hesitate on paper but not on the map, or vice versa? | |
| What was the gap between the paper sketch and the Google Earth trace? | |
| How long was the silence before they answered the constraint question? | |
| What did they say first? | |
| What did they ask first when you revealed the project? | |
| Did present-tense narration work, or did they slip into past tense? | |
| What would you change about the protocol for the next session? | |
